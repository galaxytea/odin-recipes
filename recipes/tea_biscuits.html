Tea Biscuits

A lovely snack to go with afternoon tea, these aromatic and versatile biscuits can be made easily in minutes. Recipe makes about 12-15 biscuits.

Ingredients

  • 100g plain flour (low gluten)
  • 20g cornflour or tapioca starch
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 40g white sugar or light brown sugar
  • 1-2 tbps tea leaves of your choice. Earl Grey is excellent.
  • 50g plant-based oil. Alternatively, one can use 30g melted butter and 25g oil for a slightly richer taste.
  • 1-3 tbsp milk or milk substitute
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. If using loose leaf tea, crush the tea leaves into small pieces.
  2. Combine the dry ingredients into a large bowl.
  3. Add the oil and vanilla extract to the bowl and combine well. Add milk as needed to help the dough combine.
  4. Shape the dough into a log shape and wrap in cling film. Chill in the freezer for at least 30 minutes.
  5. Preheat the oven to 180C (160C for fan-assisted ovens).
  6. Slice through the log to make approximately 12-15 disc shapes and place on a baking tray with around 2cm between each biscuit.
  7. Bake in the oven for 15 minutes. Allow to cool completely before removing from the baking tray.

This recipe is adapted from Peaceful Cusine's Earl Grey Cookie recipe.
